About Acaprez

Return to Profile

Acaprez is an organization of eight Princeton University a cappella groups who organize "arch sings" and abide by regulations regarding the audition process and song ownership. Current members include The Nassoons, The Footnotes, The Tigerlilies, The Tigressions, The Wildcats, Roaring 20, The Katzenjammers, and The Tigertones.

The Nassoons

  • Open to freshman, sophomore, and junior low-voice (TTBB) singers of all identities
  • The oldest acapella group on campus

The Footnotes

  • Open to all freshman, sophomore, and junior men
  • Fresh, all-male a cappella group with a repertoire spanning decades and genres

The Tigerlillies

  • Open to all freshman and sophomore women
  • Princeton's first all-women acapella group

The Tigressions

  • Open to all freshman, sophomore, and junior women
  • All-women acapella group with a range of songs from old classics to modern pop

The Wildcats

  • Open to all female-identifying freshmen and sophomores
  • All-female-identifying a cappella group composed of confident, vibrant, and professional women

Roaring 20

  • Open to all freshmen and sophomores
  • Co-ed a cappella group with a dynamic repertoire and a uniquely resonant sound

The Katzenjammers

  • Open to freshmen and sophomores of all voice parts and gender identities
  • Co-ed group performing jazz, classical, sacred, and pop music

The Tigertones

  • Open to freshmen and sophomore guys
  • Princeton University's signature all-male a cappella group
