About Acaprez

Return to Profile

Acaprez is an organization of eight Princeton University a cappella groups who organize "arch sings" and abide by regulations regarding the audition process and song ownership. Current members include The Nassoons, The Footnotes, The Tigerlilies, The Tigressions, The Wildcats, Roaring 20, The Katzenjammers, and The Tigertones.

    • The Nassoons: the oldest acapella group on campus, with auditions open to freshman, sophomore, and junior low-voice (TTBB) singers of all identities
    • The Footnotes: a fresh, all-male a cappella group with a repertoire spanning decades and genres open to all freshman, sophomore, and junior men
    • The Tigerlillies: Princeton's first all-women acapella group, with auditions open to all freshman and sophomore women
    • The Tigressions: all-women acapella group with a range of songs from old classics to modern pop, open to all freshman, sophomore, and junior women
    • The Wildcats: dynamic all-female-identifying a cappella group composed of confident, vibrant, and professional women open to all female-identifying freshmen and sophomores
    • Roaring 20: co-ed a cappella group, dedicated to performing a dynamic repertoire with a uniquely resonant sound, open to all freshmen and sophomores
    • The Katzenjammers: co-ed group performing jazz, classical, sacred, and pop music, open to freshmen and sophomores of all voice parts and gender identities
    • The Tigertones: Princeton University's signature all-male a cappella group, open to freshmen and sophomore guys
